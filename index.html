A Wee Bit About Me

I started learning to code after I had my first child. It was due to a mixture of things - the pandemic, feeling isolated with a newborn, wanting to keep my brain active and pick up a new skill. I dabbled a bit in python, but the realities of looking after a small child caught up with me and I had to put it on pause for a while.

After my maternity leave, I returned to work as a doctor in children's anaesthesia, and was worried about making a drug calculation error (sleep deprivation is not good for mental arithmetic). There's no margin for error, especially with tiny humans! I wanted a way to automate these calculations. I couldn't find what I needed, so I decided it was time to give coding another go. I used a variety of resources to learn and built the app I was looking for. You'll see it below. It was a challenge but I loved it. I shared it with my boss and colleagues and they now use it in the department. I feel extra fulfilled knowing that the app benefits others. It sparked something in me that I couldn't ignore, and so I enrolled myself in Le Wagon's Web Dev bootcamp to develop my skills.

I've had a wonderful career in medicine, where I've learned to work in teams to solve complex problems, keep calm under pressure, and how to be a kind human. But in this short life I'd like to keep learning and evolving, and so I'm pursuing this new path - a unique combination of creativity and logic that I love.
